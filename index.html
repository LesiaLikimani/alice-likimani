My Family

I am a proud mother of three wonderful sons. Our home is full of love, laughter, and resilience. One of my sons, Manasseh, lives with Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD)—a progressive condition that has greatly impacted his mobility and independence. His strength, courage, and smile continue to inspire me every single day.
Despite the challenges, our family remains united and full of hope. I am also a wife, and together with my husband, we do our best to nurture a home rooted in faith, love, and understanding. My family is my anchor—and they are the reason I continue to give my all, both at home and in the community.

At work

I currently serve as the Deputy Principal at Ngong Township Primary School, where I also teach Kiswahili. With years of experience in education, I have grown from a passionate classroom teacher to a leader supporting both learners and fellow educators. My role allows me to mentor others, shape school culture, and advocate for inclusive, compassionate education.
I believe every child deserves a chance—no matter their background or circumstances—and I work each day to make our school a safe, nurturing space for all.

My passion

Service is at the heart of who I am. Beyond the classroom, I dedicate my time to volunteering and community outreach, especially for children in vulnerable situations. I have supported learners living with disabilities, those from impoverished homes, children battling drug addiction, and survivors of abuse.
My community work has taken me to courtrooms to defend abused girls, to homes where neglected children needed hope, and into partnerships with organizations like St. Joseph Community and NACADA to fight substance abuse among students.
I have organized school re-admissions, sourced medical assistance, paid school fees, and helped families with disabled children find the support they need. Every act of service—no matter how small—is a seed of change, and I am committed to planting as many as I can.
Helping others isn’t just something I do—it’s who I am.

My Services

Over the years, I’ve extended my role beyond the classroom to offer meaningful support to children, families, and the wider community. Here are some of the ways I serve:

1. Mentorship and Emotional Support

I offer one-on-one and group mentorship for learners dealing with emotional, social, or academic challenges. I walk closely with students who are at risk of dropping out, facing neglect, or struggling with confidence and motivation, helping them find direction and hope.

2. Educational Support and School Re-integration

I help children from poor backgrounds or broken homes return to school by linking them to scholarships, paying fees where possible, and offering basic school supplies. I believe every child deserves the right to education, regardless of their situation.

3. Awareness and Advocacy Talks

I participate in community forums, schools, and churches to speak on topics like child rights, gender-based violence, disability inclusion, and mental wellness. I believe education extends beyond classrooms and into the heart of the community.

4. Child Protection and Legal Assistance

I advocate for the safety of children facing abuse or neglect. I have personally followed up on abuse cases, even appearing in court to defend and support victims. I work with the Children’s Department and community organizations to ensure justice and healing.

5. Substance Abuse Intervention

Through collaboration with bodies like NACADA and Kajiado North Anti-Drugs Community, I support young learners struggling with drug and substance abuse. I guide them through counseling, follow-up, and reintegration, while educating others on prevention.

6. Community Outreach and Family Support

I support families raising children with disabilities, helping them access healthcare, therapy, and emotional encouragement. I also assist with food, clothing, and shelter for families in crisis—offering practical help with compassion and dignity.
